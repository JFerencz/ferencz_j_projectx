What is 'flow'?

Put succinctly, flow is a psychological state in which the information that enters one's awareness is congruent with his or her goals, and concentration can, as a result, 'flow' effortlessly. (paraph. 39) Flow is a highly enjoyable experience, occurring when skills and challenges are in harmony with one another, thereby circumventing both anxiety [on the one hand] and boredom [on the other].

"When a person is able to organize his or her consciousness so as to experience flow as often as possible, the quality of life is inevitably going to improve because [...] even the usually boring routines of work become purposeful and enjoyable. In flow we are in control of our [attention], and everything we do adds order to consciousness."

(Csikszentmihalyi, Flow: The Psychology of Optimal Experience 40)
